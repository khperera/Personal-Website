Advanced Rheology (Fourier Transform Rheology)

 At large input amplitudes, the output becomes non-linear.

Fourier transforms can break the nonlinear components up into harmonics relative to the input sine.

We can calculate rheologically relevant parameters from the amplitude and phase shift of each harmonic. The first and third harmonics are traditionally used.

    Strain Stiffening ratio: v3/v1 = I3/I1*sin(γ3)/sin(γ1)
    Shear Thickening ratio: e3/e1 = I3/I1*cos(γ3)/cos(γ1)
